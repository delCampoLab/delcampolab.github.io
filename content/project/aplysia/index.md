The California sea hare, *Aplysia californica*, is a well-studied model organism in neurobiology and neuroscience. Despite deep knowledge of *Aplysia*'s physiology, anatomy, ethology, and biology, little is known about its microbiome. The objective of this project, in collaboration with the National Resource for *Aplysia* at the Rosenstiel School of Marine and Atmospheric Science, is to explore for the first time the prokaryotic and microeukaryotic communities associated with this model organism using a genomic, metabarcoding approach. These data will allow us to explore how the microbiome influences behaviour, ageing, and many other characteristics associated with the basic biology of these animals.
