abstract: "Labyrinthulomycetes are heterotrophic stramenopiles that are ubiquitous in a wide range of both marine and terrestrial environments. They produce ectoplasmic networks and include ecologically important saprotrophic and parasitic organisms. We present a comprehensive molecular diversity survey of labyrinthulomycetes in multiple marine environments and provide phylogenetic reference trees for their classification."
